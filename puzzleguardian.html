The Puzzle Guardian

The Puzzle Guardian

Puzzles everywhere! A world made of jigsaw pieces

Client: Vancouver Film School
Role: Game & Level Design, Lighting & Texture Art
Tools Used: Unity, Photoshop & After Effects
Duration: 4 months



OVERVIEW


The Puzzle Guardian is a first-person puzzle adventure game set in a world made of puzzle pieces. The game was developed with Unity and has a unique art style for all characters, streets & buildings. It allows players to immerse themselves and explore many areas ranging from quiet neighbourhoods to parks.

Embark on a journey inside a fantasy environment and be prepared to face the jigsaw creatures and a few other challenges. See below a gameplay video:

The following information contains the development process of The Puzzle Guardian, including each step from concept to the final game and challenges that were faced during the realization of this project.


CONCEPT & DESIGN


One of my objectives with this project was to create something unique and transform a concept into a real game. Since I was a kid I had a passion for puzzles (physical and digital) like Rubik's cube, crosswords and jigsaw pieces. The initial idea was to select one of these physical puzzles and implement their mechanics into a digital game.

After a few concepts, we decided to create a video game where the environment is entirely made with jigsaw pieces. The enemies would be different shapes of puzzles and the goal was to survive while solving some challenges.

With the definition of our project, we began to sketch and make the textures for the game characters & world.


Puzzle enemies


In addition to the textures, Unity was being used to set up a playable build with a few elements of the game. This initial demo was different from the final product since it is visualized with another type of camera (top-down perspective).


Initial prototype

After analyzing the prototype we realized that the experience would be more unique and visually appealing if the camera changed from "top-down perspective" to a "first-person view". For this new approach, a short prototype was built with tridimensional jigsaw pieces in the scene:


New camera angle

The new camera angle allowed us to explore more elements that could be implemented. At this phase, I was designing the level and determining the player's path. It was decided that the game would follow a linear gameplay style with small open areas where the user is able to walk around.



While the game world was being designed, we were also transforming the previous 2D sketches into 3D models using Autodesk Maya. The idea was to make the enemies have a flat appearance and to be shaped like 3D puzzle pieces.



The next step was to implement the initial assets into our Unity scene. At this point the level design was complete and we developed a list of necessary objects & items to decorate the game environment.


Unity scene

The textures, models & scripts were being finalized and the game was near the alpha version. After playing the first demo, some iterations were made to improve the game mechanics and adjust the challenges. In addition, the name and logo of the project were defined: The Puzzle Guardian.

RESULT


It was finally time to implement all the remaining assets into the scene such as SFX, music, particles & effects.

I conducted a Quality Assurance test with some participants in order to guarantee that the game mechanics and playability were functioning. By observing the players, we made the final necessary changes to improve the game experience. The project was approved and The Puzzle Guardian was completed.

- Final considerations:
This was a fun project to work with and the result is a game that met our expectations. I was very satisfied with the The Puzzle Guardian's art style and the creation process helped me improve my skills with Unity Engine.
