The Puzzle Guardian

The Puzzle Guardian

Puzzles everywhere! A world made of jigsaw pieces

Client: Vancouver Film School
Role: Game & Level Design, Lighting / Texture Art
Tools Used: Unity, Photoshop & After Effects
Duration: 3 months



OVERVIEW


The Puzzle Guardian is a first-person puzzle adventure game set in a world made of puzzle pieces. The game was developed with Unity and has a unique art style for all characters, streets & buildings. It allows players to immerse themselves and explore many areas ranging from quiet neighbourhoods to parks while facing enemies: the jigsaw pieces.

The following information contains the development process of The Puzzle Guardian, including each step from concept to the final game and challenges that were faced during the realization of this project.


CONCEPT & DESIGN


One of my objectives with this project was to create something unique and transform a concept into a real game. Since I was a kid I had a passion for puzzles (physical and digital) like Rubik's cube, crosswords and jigsaw pieces. The initial idea for this project was to select one of these physical puzzles and transform it into a game. After a few iterations, we decided to create a video game where the environment is entirely made of jigsaw pieces. The enemies would be different shapes of puzzles and the goal was to survive while solving some challenges. With the definition of our concept, I began to sketch and make the textures for the game characters & world.

Puzzle enemies


In addition to the textures, I was using Unity to set up a playable build with a few elements of our game. This initial demo was different from the final product since it is visualized with another type of camera (top-down perspective).

After playing with the concept I realized that the experience would be more unique if the camera changed from "top-down perspective" to a "first-person view". In order to test the new camera I made a short prototype with the jigsaw pieces in the scene:

New camera angle

The new camera angle allowed us to explore more unique elements that could be implemented in the game. At this phase, I was designing the game's level and determining the player's path. It was decided that the game would follow a linear gameplay style with small open areas where the user is able to walk around.

While the game world was being designed, we were also transforming the previous 2D sketches into 3D models using Autodesk Maya. The idea was to make the enemies have a flat appearance and to be shaped like tridimensional puzzle pieces.

The next step was to implement the initial assets into our Unity scene. At this point the level design was complete and we developed a list of necessary objects & items to decorate the game environment.

Unity scene

The textures, models & scripts were being finalized and the game was near the alpha version. After playing the first demo, some iterations were made to improve the game mechanics and adjust the challenges. In addition, the name and logo of the project were defined: The Puzzle Guardian.

RESULT


It was finally time to implement all the remaining assets into the scene such as SFX, music, particles & effects. I conducted a Quality Assurance test with some participants in order to guarantee that the game mechanics and playability were functioning. After observing the players we made the final necessary iterations to improve the game experience. The project was approved and the Puzzle Guardian was completed.

This game is available for download here (Windows only)



- Final considerations:
This was a fun project to work with and the result is a game that met our expectations. I was very satisfied with the The Puzzle Guardian's art style and the creation process helped me improve my skills with Unity Engine.
