OVERVIEW


Cubus (Cube + bus) is a modular & autonomous transit system that allows buses to attach/detach from each other generating a unique transportation experience.

Each "cube" has a capacity of 16 people and their own set of routes in the city. The modularity permits passengers to transfer between vehicles in motion and choose the one that leads them to their desired destination. With safety and efficiency in mind, Cubus is an efficient way to move around.

INTERVIEW + RESEARCH


Cubus is a project that was developed to minimize the impact of a common issue among people's experience in Toronto's public transportation: the inefficiency of buses and trains. The initial phase was to conduct interviews in order to understand what are the major complaints that users have with the current system.

56 people were asked the following questions about the Toronto Transit Commission (TTC):

- How often do you use the TTC?

- How satisfied are you with the TTC experience?

These two questions were purposefully simple with the objective of gathering the overall percentage of TTC riders and their satisfaction with the buses and trains. A questionnaire was also used to get more specific information about their desires, needs, and complaints about the TTC.


Inneficient, crowded & slow public transportation:
A frustrating experience for TTC users

The majority of users listed inefficiency, overcrowdedness, and slowness as their major problems with the current transportation system. It was revealed that they desire more available buses on the streets and less crowded vehicles in order to get a more comfortable ride experience.

After collecting the data from the interviews, we began a more in-depth research process in order to obtain information about the situation of TTC buses. Inumerous sources stated that the problem with the TTC is real and that a large number of users are unsatisfied with the provided service. CityNews reported on their website that many buses & streetcars routes around Toronto are overcrowded.


In addition to the research, personas were created based on the common responses of interviewees.



CONCEPT


After gathering all the information from the research and interviews, we were faced with the challenge: How can we solve or minimize the problem faced by the TTC users? What are the major aspects that should be the focus of our solution? The brainstorm phase began and a diverse range of conceptual systems were proposed.
